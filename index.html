🧠 How Social Media Algorithms Work

Ever wonder why your feed knows you better than your best friend? 🤔 That's the power of metadata — data about your data! Social media algorithms analyze what you click, watch, scroll past (yes, even that awkward cat video 😼), and use bytes of information (that’s 8 bits per byte!) to create a profile about you.

These algorithms aren’t just smart — they’re *lossy* too. They compress your data in ways that throw out the extra fluff (sorry, full cat video), keeping only the juicy parts. Unlike lossless compression, which keeps everything, lossy compression says, "Do we really need 20 angles of that one TikTok dance?"

Social media also handles analog data — like the sound of your voice or a video — by converting it into digital signals. But beware of roundoff errors, where the system cuts corners and suddenly your smooth opera solo sounds like a kazoo. 🎶

So next time you’re watching a video about how to toast bread while skydiving, remember: it’s not magic, it’s math... and metadata.
